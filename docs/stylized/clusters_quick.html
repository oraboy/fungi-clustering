Caption: Dendrocollybia racemosa - Branched shanklet Not my find but a collaborative effort to collect and record this rare species. I’m thrilled to get the opportunity to witness this one. The location has been recorded, and pending permissions, a sample will be making its way to Kew Gardens to be included in their genome sequencing projects and the national collection. Found by: Nicky Freeman Collected & record by: Iona Fraser @ashdownforage The species is characterised by its unusual stem, which is covered with short lateral branches. The branches often produce spherical slimeheads of translucent conidiophores  on their swollen tips. The conidiophores produce conidia (asexual spores) by mitosis. Because the fungus can rely on either sexual or asexual modes of reproduction, fruit bodies sometimes have reduced or even missing caps. The unusual stems originate from black pea-sized structures called sclerotia. The anamorphic form of the fungus, known as Tilachlidiopsis racemosa, is missing the sexual stage of its life cycle. It can reproduce at relatively low temperatures, an adaptation believed to improve its ability to grow quickly and fruit on decomposing mushrooms. (Info via Wikimili) #allthingsfungi
